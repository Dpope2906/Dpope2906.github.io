Charles Dickens was an English novelist and social critic who wrote some of the most popular and acclaimed works of literature in the 19th century, such as Oliver Twist, A Christmas Carol, and Great Expectations. He used his novels to expose the injustices and hardships of the poor and the oppressed in Victorian society. He is considered one of the greatest writers of the English language.

Robert E. Lee was an American general and commander of the Confederate States Army during the American Civil War. He is widely respected for his military skills, leadership, and loyalty to his home state of Virginia. He opposed secession but refused to fight against his fellow Southerners. He led several successful campaigns against the Union Army, such as the Second Battle of Bull Run, the Battle of Chancellorsville, and the Battle of Fredericksburg.

Ulysses S. Grant was an American general and president who led the Union Army to victory over the Confederate States in the American Civil War. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest military commanders in history. He also served as the 18th president of the United States from 1869 to 1877, where he worked to protect the rights of African Americans and promote peace and reconstruction.

Florence Nightingale was a British nurse and reformer who is known as the founder of modern nursing. She organized and trained nurses to care for the wounded soldiers during the Crimean War, where she earned the nickname “The Lady with the Lamp”. She also improved the standards of hygiene, sanitation, and hospital administration. She is widely admired for her humanitarian work and her contributions to public health.
